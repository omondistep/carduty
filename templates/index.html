▶ Import Timing Guide
▼

Good Month — Sweet Spot

Post-Registration Birthday: The best time for your car to arrive at the port is shortly after its registration "birthday". For instance, if a car was manufactured in August 2018, importing it to arrive in September allows it to depreciate another year on paper, which lowers your KRA import duty.

Off-Peak Shipping: Shipping during off-peak months (February to April) generally means smoother logistics, less port congestion, and faster clearance times.

▲

Bad Month — Watch Out

Pre-Birthday Arrival: A vehicle arriving at the port in Mombasa before its month of manufacture or registration falls into a higher duty bracket because it is legally treated as a "younger" car, costing you more in taxes.

November & December: The year-end cutoff, pre-holiday port congestion, and festive season closures can cause long delays. If clearance drags into a new calendar year, the vehicle may violate the 8-year age rule entirely, resulting in rejection or massive penalties.

Crucial Steps

  • 1 Calculate Age Accurately: Factor in 5–6 weeks of shipping and potential port delays to ensure the vehicle clears within the 8-year age limit.
  • 2 Check the CRSP: KRA calculates taxes using the Current Retail Selling Price (CRSP). Use this calculator to see how the arrival month affects your valuation.
